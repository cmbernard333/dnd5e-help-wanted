Scanning through parts you realize they are written in different languages. Common, elvish, dwarvish, bits in halfling. Most of it makes very little sense. You find a couple of passages that stand out and are written in common.

  • Entry 1

    • Wasted. Weird. Wrong. I can travel between dimensions in the material plane and the astral plane but I can't travel to the far away places in the stories of my youth. Have to try again.

  • Entry 4

    • Almost there. I have created a portal to one of my favorite childhood stories The Very Hungry Purple Worm, but this is simple. Juvenile. More complex works require greater magic!

  • Entry 7

    • Books! That is the key. Books! Books contain words. They contain the stories. I need a tome. A tome to contain the stories, the worlds, the complexities!

  • Entry 12

    • The Tome has more worlds that I have ever seen. I've experienced so many things. Reality struggles to compare to these vast tapestries!

  • Entry 18

    • The Tome is alive. It first spoke with the voice of Leonard in Of Mimics and Men. Many voices came to follow.

  • Entry 25

    • My mind is far too glossed with the experiences I have shared here. I wish to retire to something bizarre and worldly and wonderful. The Tome suggests places now. It gives me worlds that it creates, but they lack the same imagination as mortal authors. I shall retire soon and no longer live in this boring plane.

    Flipping through passages in the back of the book you find a set of rings fall out.
