Journal of Gildras Hornleaf

This journal contains many descriptions and drawings of how to properly attend to gardens, shrubs, and trees. Following that some idle recollections of important rituals and ceremonies in the cathedral. Toward its end is a set of more recent entries dating up to about 50 years ago.

  • Midsummer

    • There is a horrible howling, like many wolves on the prowl. The townsfolk and those that frequent the cathedral talk of it often and how it seems so strange for the wolves in the area. This is much less concerning to me than the bandit attacks that have come at a greater frequency than the past few seasons. Alden is not worried though, and by his guiding hand I believe we can weather whatever is to come. Helm watch over us.

  • Fall

    • Alden came back more shaken than I have seen him. He holds his sword arm high in his campaign against the bandits and the wolves, but I know his resolve is being tested. His wounds give way to his gradual failings of protecting us. If these attacks worsen, will he be able to protect us? Helm watch over us.

  • Winter

    • The wolves are among us. I see so many of the faces I have seen in town. Who is friend and who is foe? There was a great battle with howling and blood shed and now Alden is nowhere to be seen; however I know he is among them. From my hiding place here I heard others say Alden is headed back into town, but for what purpose?
